Why Custody Matters

Currency gains institutional meaning only through governed transitions.

Enterprise finance does not treat currency as a single static flag on a transaction. The same monetary event can carry multiple identities — quotation, obligation, settlement, account custody, ledger maintenance, reporting presentation, group consolidation, and audit scrutiny.

Without naming those transitions, finance organizations inherit fragmentation: reconciliation burden, FX distortion, close-cycle friction, and audit exposure long before misconduct enters the picture.

Custody is the institutional discipline through which movement becomes record — not magically at posting time, but through deliberate assignment, reconciliation, and defensibility across systems.

# AccountCcy.com External Evidence Registry

## Document Status

This document records external evidence signals for AccountCcy.com.

It starts intentionally empty except for evidence categories and recording rules.

The purpose is to avoid inflated authority claims while preparing the asset to track real recognition over time.

---

## 1. Registry Purpose

Inevitability is not declared.

It is earned through external evidence:

- citations
- backlinks
- professional mentions
- qualified usage
- diagnostic usage signals
- framework references
- institutional inquiries
- newsletter or report demand
- credible discussions in finance, ERP, audit, or accounting contexts

This registry records those signals when they are verifiable.

---

## 2. Evidence Categories

### Backlinks

Record professional or relevant links pointing to AccountCcy.com.

Fields:

- source URL
- source type
- target URL
- date discovered
- relevance
- notes

### Citations or Mentions

Record references in articles, newsletters, professional forums, presentations, or documentation.

Fields:

- source
- context
- quoted or paraphrased concept
- date discovered
- confidence level

### Diagnostic Usage Signals

Record aggregated, privacy-safe usage signals only.

Fields:

- date range
- page views
- completion events if available
- report inquiries
- privacy note

No personal data should be recorded in this file.

### Institutional Inquiries

Record serious inquiries without exposing sensitive private details.

Fields:

- inquiry type
- organization category
- date
- strategic fit
- follow-up status

### Framework Adoption Signals

Record evidence that a professional, organization, or product references AccountCcy terminology or methodology.

Fields:

- term referenced
- source
- context
- date
- quality of signal

---

## 3. Evidence Quality Levels

### Strong

Professional or institutional source, relevant context, durable link or document, clear reference to AccountCcy or its concepts.

### Moderate

Relevant but less authoritative source, useful discussion, or non-institutional mention with clear context.

### Weak

Unverified, casual, low-context, or ambiguous mention.

Weak signals may be recorded internally but should not support public authority claims.

---

## 4. Current Evidence Register

No external evidence signals are recorded yet.

This is intentional.

AccountCcy.com should not claim external authority before external authority exists.

---

## Final Evidence Rule

Do not inflate evidence.

A small verified signal is stronger than a large unsupported claim.

CCY State Chain · State 06

Reporting Currency

Reporting currency is the currency in which financial results are presented for management review, regulatory filings, investor communications, or internal performance packs. State 06 is where translation policy visibly reshapes enterprise narratives built from ledger truth.

Presentation Layer

How leadership sees performance after translation.

Ledger balances may remain stable while reporting packs swing because of methodology choices, equity carve-outs, hyperinflation contexts, or hybrid reporting regimes. CFO offices experience State 06 as bridge schedules, translation adjustments, and commentary obligations.

Custody Question

Which presentation currency tells the authoritative story to governance bodies — and does it trace cleanly to ledger custody?

Position in Chain

Reporting Currency inside the chain of custody.

Reporting currency consumes disciplined ledger output from State 05 and hands coherent packages toward consolidation engines at State 07. Confusing reporting currency with account currency invites boards to misread operational exposure versus presentation noise.
